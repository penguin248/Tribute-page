Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela

The first president of South Africa

Here's the tribute of Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela

  • 1918: In the small village of Mvezo, South Africa, Nelson Rolihlahla Mandela was born on July 18th. Little did the world know that this child would grow up to become a symbol of hope and resilience.
  • 1943: Mandela joined the African National Congress (ANC) and embarked on a lifelong journey to combat racial discrimination, setting the stage for his leadership in the fight against apartheid.
  • 1961: Nelson Mandela helped establish Umkhonto we Sizwe, the armed wing of the ANC, as peaceful resistance gave way to a more militant struggle.
  • 1964: Sentenced to life in prison for his anti-apartheid activities, Mandela's imprisonment became a global symbol of resistance to oppression.
  • 1990: After 27 years in prison, Nelson Mandela was released, signaling a new era of hope and reconciliation in South Africa.
  • 1993: Mandela, along with F.W. de Klerk, received the Nobel Peace Prize for their efforts to peacefully dismantle apartheid and establish a multiracial democracy.
  • 1994: In a historic election, Nelson Mandela became South Africa's first black president, ending apartheid and championing unity and reconciliation.
  • 1999: Stepping down as president, Mandela ensured a peaceful transition of power and continued his work for global peace and human rights.
  • 2013: On December 5th, the world bid farewell to Nelson Mandela. His legacy lives on, inspiring generations to stand up for justice and equality.

    • Nelson Mandela's life is a testament to the enduring power of resilience, forgiveness, and the relentless pursuit of justice. His legacy continues to shine as a beacon of hope for all.
